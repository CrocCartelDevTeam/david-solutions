Chimney Sweeping: How Often, How Much, and Why It Matters

June 9, 2026·5 min read

If you enjoy a cozy fire on a cool Florida evening, your chimney needs the same care as any other. Every fire leaves behind creosote - a flammable, tar-like residue that is the leading cause of chimney fires. Here is what every fireplace owner should know.

How often should a chimney be swept?

At least once a year is the standard recommendation, ideally before you start using your fireplace for the season. If you burn frequently throughout the cooler months, you may need a mid-season cleaning to keep creosote in check.

What affects the cost?

  • The level of creosote buildup (heavier buildup takes more work).
  • The height and accessibility of the chimney.
  • Whether the cap and crown need attention.
  • Whether an inspection is included.

What a professional sweep includes

A proper chimney sweep removes creosote and soot from the flue, firebox, and smoke chamber, and includes a check of the cap and crown for damage or animal nests. We seal off the work area and use HEPA vacuums so your home stays clean.

Why it matters

A clean chimney burns safer, drafts better, and protects your family from chimney fires and carbon monoxide. Skipping it to save money is a gamble that is simply not worth it.
